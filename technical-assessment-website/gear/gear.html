Gear

White Uniform

In fencing, the three disciplines (foil, saber, and epee) share the same white uniform:

  • Jacket
  • Knickers
  • Underarm protector (plastron)
  • Knee high socks
  • Chest protectors (mandatory for all female fencers but optional for male fencers)

The images include a fencing jacket, knickers, underarm protector, and female chest protector. Male chest protectors are flatter and optional.

Discipline Specific Gear

Each weapon has gear that is used specifically in that discipline.

Foil

  • Foil
    The weapon used in foil, a foil, is more lightweight than the other weapons. There is a button on the tip of the blade which needs to be clicked in order for a point to be scored. The bell guard is also the smallest compared to the three weapons. This is because the hand and arms are not target area, so the bell guard does not need to protect anything.
  • Glove
    In foil and epee, the glove is the same. It is worn on just the fencer's dominant hand and does not have any conductive area. It is used to protect the hand.
  • Mask
    Since the bib of the mask covers part of the torso, which is target area, that area of the mask is conductive. This means that the bib has the same silver, conductive material of the lame.
  • Lame
    The lame is the silver, conductive jacket that covers just the target area. In foil, it just covers the torso.
  • Soft Chest Protector
    In foil specifically, the chest protector needs to be covered in a soft material. This is to prevent foils from deflecting off of the hard surface of a chest protector.

The guards for foils are small, and do not protect the hand as that is not target area

Saber

The guards for saber are larger and part of it extends down to the end of the handle. This is to protect the wrist and to allow the fencer's hand to slide down the handle more easily to gain further extension.

  • Saber
    The weapon used in saber is a saber. Since points can be scored with any part of the saber, not just the tip, the weapon is used for slashing most often. The guard is big and rounded, which protects the wrist since it is valid target area.
  • Glove
    The glove in saber is mostly the same as the glove in foil and epee. The only difference is that the wrist has a conductive area on it since it is target area.
  • Mask
    Since the entire head is target area, the whole mask is conductive.
  • Lame
    The silver, conductive lame covers both the torso and the sleeves.

Epee

  • Epee
    The epee is slightly heavier than a foil and has a large guard to protect the hand, which is target area. Just as with foil, you score touches in epee by using the tip of the blade. There is a button on the end of an epee blade that needs to be clicked for a touch to register.
  • Glove
    The glove in epee is the same as the foil glove. Since the full body is target area, none of the equipment has the silver, conductive material on it. Instead, a touch always registers as a point, no matter where it lands.
  • Mask
    Similarly to the epee glove, masks have no conductive area on them.
  • Lame
    No lame is worn in epee.

The guard for epee is very large so that it protects the hand, which is target area.
